What will I be working on?

I plan on splitting up my time into three focuses, all of which I have great excitement for and expect to greatly aid my development. These three fields are as follows:

Maine Boys to Men Internship

To continue to explore my passion of having productive conversations surrounding ending gender-based violence, I decided to pursue an internship with nonprofit organization Maine Boys to Men, based in Falmouth, Maine. I was involved with the organization throughout high school and in my first year of college, and I am using my leave of absence to pursue my involvement with them to a greater extent than I ever have before. I am presently assisting the organization in moving their curriculum online, helping inform a local public school system on the best way to implement new sexual harassment policies, and providing feedback on existing curricula surrounding gender-based violence.

Developing machine learning model for Union College Admissions

Continuing my summer research project from the summer of 2020, I am working with Union College's Admissions department to develop a machine learning model based on their application data. The model is built to predict the probability of enrollment for a given applicant given the information they included when applying to the College, how many times they visited campus, etc. These predictions are, in turn, used to predict the incoming class profile to forecast the enrollment and finances of that class.

This project will be used as a reference point in the College's 2021 Admissions cycle, and my work this fall is focused on refining the model for use. Specifically, I am continuing to improve the model's predictive performance and preparing an automation system that will run the model and produce a class profile on a weekly basis as the College receives more applicants.
